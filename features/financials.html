What's inside

Run the restaurant on the numbers

Computed prime cost

COGS from received invoices plus labor from punches — the one number that decides whether a restaurant makes money, updated continuously.

Daily flash

Sales, covers, labor % and prime cost for the day, on the dashboard, the moment the last check closes.

Live 30-day P&L

A rolling profit-and-loss you can open any day of the month — not a report you wait for the books to close to see.

GL-categorized AP

Invoices land as accounts payable, mapped to GL categories, so accounting isn't re-keying receipts from a shoebox.

Budget vs. actual

Set category budgets and watch actuals against them on business dates — catch the drift with two weeks left, not after.

Export & GL sync

CSV export always, plus an optional GL journal push to your accounting middleware and a data-warehouse feed on Enterprise.

Budget vs. actual

See the month drifting in time to fix it

Every category — food, labor, the controllables — tracked against budget on the same business-date basis as your sales, so a bad trend is a conversation this week, not a surprise next month.

  • Category budgets vs. actual, updated daily
  • Business-date accurate — 1:30 a.m. checks land on the right day
  • Drill from a category straight to the invoices behind it

One source of truth

The numbers are a by-product of running service

You don't enter data to get a P&L — closing checks, receiving invoices and clocking in is the data. The financials are always as current as the last thing that happened on the floor.
