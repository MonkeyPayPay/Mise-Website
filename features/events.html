Events & Private Dining

From first inquiry to a real event P&L

Run private dining like the profit center it is: a lead pipeline from inquiry to booked, a BEO editor, a production sheet and staffing plan, and deposits by card. And because the costs already live in Mise, every booking shows a true event P&L — not a guess.

What's inside

The whole event, in one place

Lead pipeline

Every inquiry becomes a tracked lead — contacted, proposal sent, booked — so nothing falls through and you know your close rate.

BEO editor & print

Build the Banquet Event Order — menu, timeline, room, AV, notes — and print it for the kitchen and the floor.

Production sheet

The kitchen's pull-and-prep list for the event, generated from the BEO so nothing gets missed at load-in.

Staffing plan

Assign the team the event needs — and those hours flow straight into the labor forecast so the schedule already accounts for it.

Deposits & card-on-file

Hold a deposit or keep a card on file through Stripe; the guest enters it securely and you store only the token.

A true event P&L

Revenue and costs are tagged to the event, so you see the actual margin on the buyout — something a standalone events tool simply can't.

The BEO

One document the whole team runs on

The BEO and production sheet are generated from the same booking, so the sales manager, the chef and the captain are working from one source of truth — printable, and always current.

  • Menu, timeline, room layout, AV and notes in one BEO
  • Production sheet auto-built for the kitchen
  • Client proposals with e-signature to close the booking

One source of truth

Events aren't a bolt-on — they're in the P&L

Because events share Mise's cost data, staffing and payments, an event's revenue and margin land in the same live P&L as the rest of the restaurant. Private dining finally pays for itself, on paper.
