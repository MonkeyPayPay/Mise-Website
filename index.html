The problem

Seven tools that don't talk to each other

A POS for sales. A separate app for reservations. Another for scheduling. A spreadsheet for ordering. A different login for accounting. Nightly exports. Re-keyed invoices. And nobody's quite sure which spreadsheet is current. Every seam between those tools is where money leaks and service breaks down.

The platform

Eight modules. One source of truth.

Every module writes to the same live data. Change one thing and the whole restaurant knows.

Inventory

Live depletion from POS sales, moving-average costing, plate & recipe costing, waste logs and variance counts — with auto-86 the moment an ingredient hits zero.

Replaces Toast · Lightspeed · Lavu

Reservations & Guests

A table-availability engine, waitlist with quoted times, and a guest CRM that builds itself — visits, lifetime spend, allergies and VIP tags, auto-merged by phone and email.

Scheduling & Labor

Draft → publish schedules, a shift-swap marketplace with manager approval, time-off, a time clock, and overtime flagged before it's worked against live labor % vs sales.

How it works

One closed check moves the entire restaurant

A closed check on your POS is a single webhook to Mise. That one event does four things at once — no nightly exports, no re-keying.

  1. 1

    Sales update instantly

    The check lands in today's flash and the live P&L the second it closes.

  2. 2

    Inventory depletes

    Recipe costing draws down every ingredient — and auto-86s a dish the moment one hits zero.

  3. 3

    The floor is told

    The 86 posts to #kitchen so the team finds out from the app, not from an angry expo.

  4. 4

    The guest profile grows

    Visit count and lifetime spend update, with an auto-"regular" tag at five visits.

All reporting runs on business dates, not calendar dates — a check closed at 1:30 a.m. lands on the previous day's numbers, controlled per location.

Questions

The details

Do I have to replace my POS?

No. Mise sits on top of the POS you already run. Webhook adapters for Toast, Square and Lightspeed turn a closed check into one normalized event that fans out to every module — inventory, financials, guest CRM and the 86 board.

Are there per-cover or per-reservation fees?

Never. Reservations and the guest booking widget are flat-rate on the Service tier and up. You pay per location, not per guest who walks through the door.

How long does it take to get set up?

Most single locations are live within a day. Your menu, recipes and vendor catalogs import once, and the platform boots with your real sales history flowing through the sync engine.

Can staff see the financials?

Only if you let them. Roles are enforced server-side: managers get the full platform, while servers, hosts and cooks get a role-scoped view — no financials, no vendor costs, no colleagues' wages.

What does it cost to try?

Every tier includes a 14-day free trial with unlimited staff accounts. No card tricks, no per-cover fees.
