Security & trust

Your restaurant’s data, handled with care

Mise holds sales, wages, guest details and your P&L — the most sensitive data in your business. Here’s how we protect it, in plain language, and where we’re honest about what’s still ahead.

How Mise protects you

The controls behind the platform

Encryption in transit & at rest

All traffic is served over HTTPS. Sensitive personal data — guest contact details and the like — is encrypted at rest with an operator-held key (MISE_DATA_KEY), so a database snapshot alone never exposes PII in the clear.

We never store card numbers

Card data is tokenized by Stripe. Mise stores only the token needed to charge a deposit or a card on file — the full card number never touches our servers, which keeps your PCI scope small.

Role-based access, enforced server-side

Managers get the full platform; servers, hosts and cooks get a role-scoped view — no financials, no vendor costs, no colleagues’ wages. Roles are enforced on the server, not hidden in the UI.

SSO & multi-factor auth

Connect your identity provider over OpenID Connect, and enforce TOTP-based MFA on manager and operator accounts — so access follows the accounts you already govern.

Tenant isolation

Every record is scoped to your organization through a single enforced data chokepoint. One restaurant group can never read another’s data, even on shared infrastructure.

Your data is yours

Export your data at any time — sales, inventory, guests, financials — as CSV. No lock-in, no ransom for your own history. Deletion on request.

Audit trails

Sensitive actions — voids, comps, price changes, permission changes — are recorded with who and when, so there’s always an accountable record.

Resilient hosting & backups

Runs on managed cloud infrastructure with a managed Postgres database and regular backups, so your operating data survives a bad day.

Where we are today

Encryption, tokenized payments, server-side RBAC, SSO/MFA, tenant isolation and audit trails are built into the platform. The public demo runs in a sandbox with integrations dark and no real guest data.

What’s on the roadmap

A formal third-party audit (SOC 2 Type II) and a published data-processing agreement are on our roadmap as we onboard our first production restaurants. We’d rather tell you that plainly than imply a certification we don’t yet hold.

Responsible disclosure

If you believe you’ve found a security issue, please email us before disclosing it publicly. We read every report and will work with you in good faith.
