About Mise

We put the whole restaurant in its place

Mise is an all-in-one restaurant operating system — POS, reservations, inventory, scheduling, procurement, financials and events — built on a simple conviction: a restaurant runs best when its tools are one system, not seven.

Try the live demo Book a demo

Why we built it

Seven logins, none of them agreeing

Walk into most restaurants and you’ll find a POS for sales, a separate app for reservations, another for scheduling, a spreadsheet for ordering, and a different login for accounting — with nightly exports and re-keyed invoices stitching them loosely together. Every seam between those tools is where money leaks and service breaks down. Mise removes the seams by removing the separate tools: it’s one platform where the sale, the guest, the inventory and the P&L are the same data, updated in the same second.

What we believe

The principles behind the product

Everything in its place

Mise en place is the discipline every great kitchen runs on — every tool, every ingredient, ready before service. We built the software the same way: one place for the whole operation, ready when you are.

One system, on purpose

The restaurant stack fractured into a dozen apps that don’t talk. We believe the opposite: the POS, the floor, the pantry, the schedule and the P&L should be one system, because a closed check touches all of them at once.

Honest by default

No per-cover fees. No hidden add-on tiers for the things a restaurant obviously needs. Clear pricing, your data exportable at any time, and a security page that says plainly what we do and don’t yet have.

Built with operators

Mise is shaped by the realities of a busy floor — 1:30 a.m. checks landing on the right business date, an 86 that reaches the line in the same second, overtime caught before it’s worked. Details only operators care about, because they’re the ones who feel them.

Who’s behind it

A small team, close to the floor

Mise is built by a small, operator-minded team led by founder Jordan O’Brien. We’re early, we’re honest about it, and we’d rather earn a handful of restaurants that love the product than sell a logo. If that sounds like the partner you want, we’d love to talk.
