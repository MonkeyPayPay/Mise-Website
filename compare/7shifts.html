Why teams switch

Labor is a line on the P&L, not a separate app

Labor % without an integration

7shifts reads your sales through a POS connector. In Mise the sale is in the system, so labor % vs. sales is exact and instant — nothing to connect or reconcile.

Overtime caught before it's worked

Mise flags a shift that pushes someone into OT as you build the week — and that cost lands directly in prime cost, not just a labor report.

Event-aware forecasting

A booked 120-guest private event shows up as demand in the labor forecast, because events live in the same system — a standalone scheduler can't see your BEOs.

One login for the manager

The same screen that runs the schedule runs inventory counts, the P&L and the floor. Managers don't bounce between a scheduler, a POS and an accounting tool.

An honest take

Which one is right for you

When 7shifts fits

7shifts is an excellent choice if you're happy with your current POS and want a focused, polished, best-of-breed scheduling and team-communication app that integrates on top of it.

When Mise fits

Mise fits when you want scheduling to be part of the whole operating picture — labor measured against a live P&L, overtime hitting prime cost, and event hours in the forecast — all on the system that already runs service.
