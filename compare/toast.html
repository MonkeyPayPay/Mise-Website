Why teams switch

What you stop paying for — and stop reconciling

One bill, not six add-ons

Inventory, scheduling, reservations, events and reporting are in the platform — not a menu of upsells that each carry their own login and their own monthly line item.

A P&L that's actually live

Because COGS comes from your invoices and labor from your punches, Mise computes prime cost continuously. No nightly export to a separate accounting product to see if you made money.

No hardware lock-in

Run on the tablets you already have. Nothing about Mise requires a proprietary terminal, so you're not re-buying hardware to switch or to add a station.

Inventory the POS actually feeds

Every sale depletes its recipe at moving-average cost and 86s a dish the moment it hits zero — the pantry and the POS are the same system, not two that sync overnight.

An honest take

Which one is right for you

When Toast fits

Toast is a strong fit if you want a purpose-built POS with a large hardware ecosystem and third-party marketplace, and you're comfortable buying (and integrating) inventory, payroll and analytics as separate add-ons or partners.

When Mise fits

Mise fits if you'd rather run the POS and the entire back office on one data model — where inventory, labor and the P&L update from the same closed check — without stitching add-ons together or paying per module.
