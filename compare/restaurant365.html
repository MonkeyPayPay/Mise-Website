Why teams switch

The back office, without the integration seams

No POS import to reconcile

R365 is only as current as its last POS sync. In Mise the sale and the accounting entry are the same event, so prime cost never lags a day behind the floor.

Front-of-house included

Reservations, a live floor plan, a guest CRM and events aren't in R365's scope at all. In Mise they're part of the same P&L and the same guest record.

One implementation, not two

You're not integrating a POS to an accounting suite and maintaining that pipe. It's one system to onboard, one to train on, one to support.

Right-sized for a single venue too

R365's accounting depth shines across big multi-concept groups. Mise gives an independent or small group the same prime-cost discipline without that weight.

An honest take

Which one is right for you

When Restaurant365 fits

Restaurant365 is the right call for larger multi-concept groups that need deep restaurant accounting standardized across many locations running a mix of existing POS systems, and who have the resources to run those integrations.

When Mise fits

Mise fits when you'd rather not run two systems: the POS that captures the sale and the back office that accounts for it are the same product, so prime cost and the P&L are live by construction — ideal for independents and small groups.
